His career trajectory – from a budding stand-up comedian during his Saturday Night Live (SNL) days to one of the most bankable stars in the industry – speaks to his unparalleled talent and unwavering work ethic, which has seen him feature in over 56 films that have grossed over $3.8 billion in the United States and Canada alone, and over $6.7 billion worldwide.

Eddie Murphy's Stand-Up Roots

Eddie Murphy’s journey to stardom began with a stand-up performance at the Roosevelt Youth Center when he was just 15 years old.

This performance led to work at other comedy clubs in New York City and eventually to a spot on Saturday Night Live.

On SNL, Murphy quickly became one of the show’s most popular cast members, with his characters like Gumby, Mr. Robinson, and Velvet Jones becoming instant fan favorites.

During Murphy’s time on SNL in 1980, he was paid $4,500 per episode. His salary increased the following season, and he began receiving $30,000 per episode — which was unusual then, especially for a Black actor.

His stand-up specials, Delirious and Eddie Murphy Raw, were both huge successes, with Raw grossing $50 million and becoming the highest-grossing concert film at the time.

In 1982, the Emmy-Award-winning actor made his big-screen debut in the buddy cop action comedy film 48 Hrs, which received critical acclaim and was considered by many as one of the best films of the year:

It was also wildly successful at the box office, earning over $78 million, a very lucky initial film foray for Murphy.

48 Hrs. was released on December 10th 1982 , and grossed around $78.8 million in its run.

Adjusting for inflation, $78.8 million is equivalent to $248.4 million in late 2023.

Murphy’s early career was a remarkable success. He was able to parlay his stand-up success into a successful film career, and he became one of the biggest stars of the 1980s.

His first films – including 48 Hrs., Beverly Hills Cop, and Trading Places – were all huge hits, and he became one of the most sought-after actors in Hollywood.

Comedy King To Box Office Titan

Eddie Murphy's transition from stand-up comedy to Hollywood stardom was marked by a string of lucrative film deals.

As one of the most sought-after actors at the time, Murphy drew the audience to the theaters and, as such, commanded a lot in salaries for projects. He earned $1 million for his role in 1984's Best Defense, a hefty sum at the time.

His breakthrough came later that year with Beverly Hills Cop, for which he was paid a then-unprecedented $14 million (comprising $4 million in salary and an additional $10 million from back-end deals).

The film's success spawned two sequels, with Murphy netting $8 million and $15 million from each respectively.

In 1988, Murphy earned international acclaim when he starred in the romantic comedy film Coming To America.

The film was a hit both locally and worldwide; it made over $128 million in the United States and ended up with a global total of $288 million.

While the exact figure is undisclosed, it is reported that Murphy earned a significant sum of between $15 and $20 million for Coming To America.

For 1996’s The Nutty Professor, Eddie Murphy is said to have been paid $16 million.

For its sequel, 2000’s Nutty Professor II: The Klumps, Murphy was paid $20 million, plus 20 percent of the film’s gross receipts, which brought his total amount earned from the film to over $60 million.

A master of many crafts, Murphy has also lent his voice to animated characters.

The actor made his voice acting debut in Disney's Mulan, for which he was reportedly paid $14 million.

He also voiced Thurgood Stubbs in The PJs and Donkey in the Shrek series.

The Finances Behind Eddie Murphy's Fame

In addition to picking up big paychecks in Hollywood, Eddie Murphy boosts his bank balance through business deals and endorsements, which has seen his net worth hit $200 million.

Murphy's real estate portfolio is impressive; it includes properties in New York, California, and New Jersey. His most notable property is his custom-built mansion in Beverly Hills, valued at an estimated $20 million.

He acquired the 3.7-acre lot in 2001 for $10 million and invested heavily in renovations, resulting in a sprawling 18,598-square-foot estate.

Murphy's mansion boasts ten bedrooms, 17 bathrooms, a tennis court, and a four-car garage.

In 2007, Murphy purchased the 15-acre Rooster Cay island in the Bahamas for $15 million. He also formerly owned another Bahamian property called Pearl Island.

His New Jersey estate, equipped with a bowling alley, marble fireplaces, a private recording studio, and a spa, was sold to musicians Alicia Keys and Swizz Beatz in 2012 for $12 million.

Murphy's music career has also contributed to his wealth. He has released several successful albums, including Party All The Time With The Murphy Family, Love Signals, and Comedian, which earned him a Grammy Award for "Best Comedy Album."

Murphy has also capitalized on his celebrity status through lucrative endorsement deals with brands such as PepsiCo and Toyota, appearing in an advertisement for the automobile company in 2012.

In 2019, Murphy reportedly signed a $70 million deal with Netflix to produce a series of comedy specials, and in late 2023, he starred in the Christmas-themed filmCandy Cane Lane.
