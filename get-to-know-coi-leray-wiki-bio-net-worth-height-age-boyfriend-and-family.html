Coi Leray, born Brittany Collins on May 11, 1997, is a multifaceted American personality known for her successful career as a rapper, singer, songwriter, and social media influencer. She hails from Boston, Massachusetts, USA, and is notably recognized as the daughter of the prominent American television personality, Raymond Leon Scott, famous as “Benzino”.

Leray has made a mark with her music albums like Everythingcoz, EC2, and Now or Never, along with several EPs and singles. Her controversial single “No More Parties” stirred up a family dispute in March 2021 due to some contentious lyrics about her father.

Wiki/Biography

Born on May 11, 1997, in Boston, Massachusetts, USA, Coi Leray comes from a family deeply ingrained in the spotlight. She is known to follow the Christian faith and is currently 26 years old. Coi’s birth name is Brittany Collins, and she made the bold decision to drop out of high school at the age of 16 to pursue her passion for music. This early dedication has brought her widespread recognition as one of America’s most esteemed rappers and singers.

Career in Music

Starting her musical journey at a young age, Coi Leray’s decision to leave school at 16 paved the way for her flourishing music career.
