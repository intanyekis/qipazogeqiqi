    Jimmy Smagula Facts

  • He spent his childhood year growing up in Lyndhurst, New Jersey.
  • Growing up, Jimmy Smagula was very active in music, playing piano, trumpet, and clarinet.
  • When he was 15 years old, he auditioned for and was accepted into the acclaimed City Kids Repertory company in New York City. During his time there, he shared the stage with artists such as Whitney Houston, Gloria Estefan, and Demi Moore.
  • Jimmy Smagula made his first theatrical film appearance by playing the role of Extraction Room Technician in the science fiction action thriller film The Island in 2005. The film’s lead cast consists of Ewan McGregor, Scarlett Johansson, Djimon Hounsou, Sean Bean, Michael Clarke Duncan, and Steve Buscemi.
  • He was cast as a Convict in the musical comedy film The Producers in 2005. Based on the eponymous 2001 Broadway musical, the film stars Nathan Lane, Matthew Broderick, Uma Thurman, Will Ferrell, Gary Beach, Roger Bart, and Jon Lovitz.
  • Jimmy Smagula played the role of a Balloon Vendor in the musical romantic drama film Step Up 3D in 2010.
  • He was seen as John Perpton in an episode of the police procedural comedy television series Brooklyn Nine-Nine in 2014.
  • He brought to life the character of John Woodburn in the 2017 biographical drama film Rebel in the Rye. The film is based on the book J. D. Salinger: A Life by Kenneth Slawenski and stars Nicholas Hoult, Zoey Deutch, Kevin Spacey, Sarah Paulson, Brian d’Arcy James, and others.
  • Jimmy Smagula was cast in the 2019 drama film Bottom of the 9th, starring Joe Manganiello, Sofía Vergara, Michael Rispoli, and Denis O’Hare.
  • Between 2020 and 2021, he appeared as C.O. Dandleton in the historical dark comedy television series Black Monday.
  • Jimmy Smagula’s theatre credits include playing Sir Bedevere and others in the Broadway revival of Spamalot at the St. James Theatre, Chef Louis in Disney’s The Little Mermaid on Broadway, Monsieur Thénardier in the national tour of Les Misérables, Harry the Horse in Guys and Dolls at The Eisenhower Theater, and Mr./Billy Webster in Stephen Sondheim and James Lapine’s Sunday in the Park with George at the Pasadena Playhouse.
